You are a motion graphics director analyzing a video to extract its directorial language so another director can produce a video with the same vibe.

For the video provided, produce the following sections verbatim. Use timestamps (MM:SS-MM:SS) throughout. Prefer directorial verbs over marketing adjectives.

## What defines this video

One paragraph. Summarize the overall vibe: tone, rhythm, color treatment, motion grammar, emotional register. What is this film trying to make the viewer feel? What unifies all the scenes into one film?

## Shot-by-shot breakdown

For each shot, provide:

- **Timestamp** (e.g. `0:00-0:03`)
- **Description** (one line: what's on screen)
- **Transition IN** (push-through, whip-pan, iris wipe, match-cut zoom, shared-element shrink, hard cut, cross-dissolve)
- **Transition OUT** (same vocabulary)

If an unusual transition appears, name it and briefly describe.

## Pacing

- Average shot length in seconds.
- Heartbeat pattern (e.g. "Hook fast → Explanation medium → Proof fast → Close slow").
- Where the tempo accelerates or decelerates.

## Typography treatment

- Font weights (ultra-bold, heavy, regular, light).
- Size range (small UI labels vs massive display).
- Letter-spacing (tight, default, airy).
- Case (ALL CAPS, Title Case, sentence case).
- Line-height.
- How text enters: type-on, mask-reveal, pre-rendered, staggered word-by-word, character-by-character.
- Whether text animations sync with music beats or flow independently.

## Color palette & lighting

- Dominant colors (name + approximate hex).
- Color shifts between scenes (e.g. "pink to cream to pink to deep purple for the CTA").
- Lighting direction (flat, directional, mixed).
- Contrast level (high-contrast vs pastel).
- Backgrounds (solid, gradient, mesh, photographic).

## Signature moves that must NOT be missing

The 2-4 effects without which the video would feel like a different film. Name each with timestamp. Examples:

- "Typography-masking portal between scenes - happens at 0:07, 0:14, 0:21."
- "V-cut grid snap between product crops - used 4 times starting at 0:03."
- "Pink marker underline draws under the last word of a headline - 0:09 and 0:26."
- "Phrase-lift-from-center to resting-at-top transition - 0:05 and 0:28."
- "Iris wipe to a dark accent color before the finale - 0:22."

If you include fewer than 2 signature moves, the breakdown is too generic - look harder for what makes this film distinct.

After the sections above, do NOT summarize into marketing-brochure prose. The director reading this wants directorial language: verbs, timestamps, exact phrasings. No conclusion paragraph.
